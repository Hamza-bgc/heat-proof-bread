Why your bread hardens

01 — Hydration
An under-hydrated
dough dries out faster
Heat changes how flour absorbs water. Nudge your hydration slightly higher to keep the crumb soft for longer.
01
02 — Fermentation
Controlled,
not accelerated
Fermentation that moves too fast leaves the dough short on structure. A well-developed gluten network holds moisture better after baking.
02
03 — Autolyse
The
underused weapon
Flour and water rest before yeast and salt go in. Better extensibility, better water retention, a softer crumb, slower staling.
03
04 — Improver
The quiet reinforcement
against the heat
A baking improver strengthens dough stability, optimizes water retention, and extends crumb freshness — a valuable ally for offsetting summer heat's impact on fermentation.
04
05 — Baking
Steam makes
all the difference
An oven that's too dry at the start hardens the crust and dries out the crumb. Critical when the surrounding air is already dry from the heat.
05
06 — Cooling
Gradual,
never abrupt
Bread cooled too quickly loses its moisture all at once. Let it rest in open air, away from direct drafts.
